Some Graphs Produced from the Data




Figure 2.1



Language Distribution for Town and Village


The distribution of languages for Towns and Villages are being displayed in figure 2.1. To get a truer understanding of this distribution the next step would be to plot these as percentages of the Towns and Villages rather than raw data. However, we can see a trend in that Towns have more English speakers than any other language and in Villages Asm was the most common. We can also see that these two languages are the most widely used for both locations.



School Type Distribution for Town and Village


Figure 2.2 has also been grouped by Town and Village to discover the distribution of school types. Again, this could benefit from being displayed as percentages to give better comparison between the two than just the raw data. We are still able to see from the graph that Government schools are the highest attended by both students from Towns and students from Villages but a higher percentage of the students from towns attend private schools - not to be confused with raw number of students as overall more students from villages attend private schools. This is due to having more students from villages present in this dataset.

Family Income Distribution


Figure 2.3 is a bar plot of family's monthly incomes grouped by town or village. It shows very few incomes in the very high bracket with many of the families belonging to the medium income bracket. We can also see the same trends of family incomes for both locations.



Final Exam Performance for Town and Village Students


Figure 2.4 has the students grouped by town or village again, this time looking at how they performed in their final exams. We could see similar trends in that the result most seen for both demographics was a good score followed by very good. It would also be worthwhile to convert these results into percentages per town and village to get a better understanding of how each result is distributed.

Figure 2.5



How does Travel Time influence Class Attendance


For figure 2.5 we produced a graph whereby the bars have been grouped by travel time and the individual bars represent the class attendance. This shows us that many of students have a small travel time. Students with small and average travel times both see the same trend of good, average then poor attendance from highest to lowest whereas those with a large travel time being average, good and then poor. A prescriptive solution to try to influence those travelling furthest to up their attendance records could be to closely monitor attendance in general and act if any issues arise. There are multiple avenues that a college could go down for this, but one option may be to push the agenda on how important attendance is for good results - something that we should be able to create proof of from the dataset.



What affect do Study Hours have on Final Exam Performance?


We have produced another grouped bar chart for this with the bars being grouped by study hours and each bar representing the final exam performance. We can see that for those students whose study hours were considered poor none of them achieved the best grade and for those whose study time was good everyone achieved higher than a pass. We could then start to consider why this may be the case. One prescriptive solution for encouraging students to understand the importance of studying could be to make these research findings available to them to hope that it makes the students aware on the benefits of putting the time in. It could also be useful to speak to some students personally to find what is holding them back in terms of studying and finding how best to support those students.
